# Project2

## Sources
Our source was the World Bank Human Development Indicators dataset (environment.csv)

## Initial Visualization
Our initial visualizations supported the proposal that the United States has caused more CO2 damage than any other country (aside from China) for a large proportion of industrialized history.
### Deceptive Techniques Used
Fig. 1 draws attention to the United States’s CO2 damage levels through the title and the usage of bold color to highlight the US’s bar. However, despite this the data actually shows that China has caused more damage overall. Fig. 2 emphasizes the time period where the United States dominated CO2 damage, which reinforces the argument while downplaying more recent trends.

## Opposing Visualizations
Our opposing visualizations supported the proposal that China’s CO2 damage has rapidly increased and is now significantly more than any other country including the United States.
### Deceptive Techniques Used
Fig. 3 only used CO2 damage data from 2019 instead of cumulative CO2 damage. However, the title keeps this vague and doesn’t mention the time range of the data. Fig. 4 uses an indexed scale, which exaggerates China’s rapid growth compared to the United States, which also emphasizes growth trends rather than absolute values, making China’s increase appear more dramatic.
